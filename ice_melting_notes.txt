To perform this calculation,  the value provided (458,500) is the total mass in kilograms (kg), as 458,500 kg/m^3 would be roughly 500 times denser than actual ice.Here is the step-by-step calculation to melt that mass of ice in exactly 2 hours, assuming the ice starts at its melting point (0 degreesCelsius).


1. Identify the Variables
- Mass (m): 458,500 kg
- Latent Heat of Fusion(L_f): 334,000 J/kg (The energy needed to melt ice at 0 degreesCelsius)
- Time (t): 2 hours = 7,200 seconds


2. Calculate Total Energy Required (Q)
Since we are focusing on the phase change (turning solid ice into liquid water), we use the latent heat formula:$$Q = m \cdot L_f$$$$Q = 458,500\text{ kg} \cdot 334,000\text{ J/kg}$$$$Q = 153,139,000,000\text{ Joules (or } 153.14\text{ GJ)}$$3. Calculate the Power ($P$)Power is the rate of energy transfer over time. To find the Watts ($W$), we divide the total Joules by the total seconds:$$P = \frac{Q}{t}$$$$P = \frac{153,139,000,000\text{ J}}{7,200\text{ s}}$$$$P \approx 21,269,306\text{ Watts}$$Final ResultTo melt $458,500\text{ kg}$ of ice in 2 hours, you would need:$\approx 21.27\text{ Megawatts (MW)}$

1. Starting Temperature: If the ice is colder than 0 C. (e.g., it's "deep freeze" ice at -15C you would need an additional $1.45 MW of power just to bring it up to the melting point before it even starts turning into water.
